World Historical Places - GERMANY

The Brandenburg Gate

Dating back to the 18th century, this monument is one of the most visited places in Germany. Prussian king Frederick William II built this gate in Berlin in a neoclassical style and today, it stands as a symbol of Berlin and German division during the Cold War. The gate is 26 meters high and the beautiful Quadriga statue designed by Johann Gottfried Schadow crowns the wall at the top. When Germany got reunified, this epic gate transformed itself into New Berlin's symbol of unity and today, draws several thousands of tourists every single day.

The Rebuilt Reichstag

One of the most significant and iconic places in Germany, the Rebuilt Reichstag is popular for its magnificent glass dome structure. The building is where the modern-day German Parliament sits and the city’s panoramic views around that the glass dome offers to its visitors are outlandish. Cyrillic graffiti in Reichstag left by Soviet soldiers are still on the walls and whisper untold tales from those days. The building is truly eco-friendly as it generates its energy. The Käfer Dachgarten Restaurant at the roof of Reichstag serves scrumptious food.

Marienplatz Square

Located centrally in the old town of Altstadt, this tourist destination is one of the best places to start your Munich tour. It was founded by the eminent Henry the Lion back in the 12th century for being utilized for tournaments, celebratory events, and for medieval markets. At present, the square still stands tall as a popular meeting point for both locals and visitors. It has been the main square since 1158 and is largely vouched as the central square for the longest time now. Being located close to the city, it is a connecting point to all the major tourist spots.

The Theatine Church of St. Cajetan

Being completed in 1690, The Theatine Church of St. Cajetan had been built commemorating the style of the Italian High Baroque. It offers a state of the art façade, an impressive 71-meter high dome with an alluring interior, where the exterior comprises varied marble statues of saints, lanterns along a lion located above the dome. The interior is attractive, with a high altar, beautiful paintings, and rare architecture that sums up the beauty of the church.

Eschenheim Tower

Built long back in the early 1400s, the tower stands tall as the finest relic from the ancient walls. This tall structure, 47 meters high, never fails to impress with the exquisite dimensions it has. Dominating the Eschenheimer Gate district, the tower houses a café, meeting rooms largely occupied by local historical groups and societies. This oldest skyscraper with over 600 years of history is also the best place in Germany for a gastronomic tour as the exclusive lava stone dishes and wines served at the lounge and outdoor seating area are the best you will find in Germany.
