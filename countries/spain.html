Royal Palace of Madrid

The royal palace of Madrid was designed to impress the world. It is the Spanish version of Versailles. But unlike the Versailles that has turned into a museum, the royal palace of Madrid serves as the official residence of the king of Monarc. Still, it is the official state for organising events. Philip V was the one who commissioned the palace in the eighteen century. It is built using granite and Colmenar stone. The facade of this beautiful place in Spain has an iconic column and doric plasters have drawings on it, which were sculpted by Bernini, a famous sculptor of that time.

Marienplatz Square

Located centrally in the old town of Altstadt, this tourist destination is one of the best places to start your Munich tour. It was founded by the eminent Henry the Lion back in the 12th century for being utilized for tournaments, celebratory events, and for medieval markets. At present, the square still stands tall as a popular meeting point for both locals and visitors. It has been the main square since 1158 and is largely vouched as the central square for the longest time now. Being located close to the city, it is a connecting point to all the major tourist spots.

The Theatine Church of St. Cajetan

Being completed in 1690, The Theatine Church of St. Cajetan had been built commemorating the style of the Italian High Baroque. It offers a state of the art façade, an impressive 71-meter high dome with an alluring interior, where the exterior comprises varied marble statues of saints, lanterns along a lion located above the dome. The interior is attractive, with a high altar, beautiful paintings, and rare architecture that sums up the beauty of the church.

Eschenheim Tower
